Diagnosis and Certification of English Competency

DCEC (Diagnosis and Certification of English Competency) is an online diagnostic system that assesses an English learner's learning efficiency. The aim of DCEC is to "simultaneously assess and certify" by providing test results that reflect an English learner's proficiency in communication. The system not only certifies a learner's English ability but also emphasizes its function to measure a learner's learning efforts by providing both vertical and horizontal diagnostic information. Vertical diagnostic information refers to a learner’s overall level of performance and how it corresponds to relevant standards, while horizontal diagnostic information consists of an index that details various aspects of a learner's current level of performance.

The design of DCEC reading comprehension assessments integrates EFL reading comprehension theory and references similar standards, indexes, and syllabi from other countries (e.g., the U.S., Canada, and Taiwan). DCEC allows test takers to identify their level of reading ability instantly and accurately as well as track their long-term progress.
