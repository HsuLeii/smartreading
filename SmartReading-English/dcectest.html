The design of DCEC reading comprehension assessments integrates EFL reading comprehension theory and references similar standards, indexes, and syllabi from other countries (e.g., the U.S., Canada, and Taiwan). DCEC allows test takers to identify their level of reading ability instantly and accurately as well as track their long-term progress.

DCEC Reading Comprehension Test Report

After completing the assessment, a diagnostics report will present the test taker's overall reading ability and their reading ability in four specific aspects: Information Extraction, Information Integration, Local Inference, and Global Inference. DCEC utilizes established norms and difficulty parameters derived from pilot testing to accurately assess the test taker’s reading level according to their test results. Upon completing the assessment, test takers will then receive reading material recommendations in line with their current reading level.
