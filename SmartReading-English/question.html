Frequently Asked Questions

Q：

Does the SRE system make use of any international standards to classify books?

A：

The SRE system first considers the norms of elementary and high school grade levels in Taiwan as its reference standard. The classification system of SRE's reader-grading technology corresponds to CEFR and Lexile. However, the platform is still in the process of integrating these standards into the system. Nevertheless, English learners can still use the system to determine the grade/level of their reading abilities and find suitable books.

Q：

What books are available on the platform?

A：

Our platform features both original e-books as well as e-books from other publishers, all of which may be directly accessed by users.

Q：

How can teachers interpret test scores?

A：

Every test report includes a score rating a student’s reading abilities and the norm average scores of students in the same grade. Teachers can view general and detailed information about their student's reading ability level by comparing the student's test scores with the norm. A relative scale of scores and grades is also available for teachers to assess the grade level of the student's reading abilities.

Q：

How long is the DCEC test?

A：

There is no time limit for the DCEC test. The test duration largely depends on the reading speed of the student. Most students complete the test in approximately 20 to 30 minutes.

Q：

If my test was interrupted due to connection issues or other reasons, will I have to retake the entire test?

A：

The test system will automatically record your progress. If the test was not completed for any reason, you can continue from the last incomplete question the next time you log into the system.

Q：

Can the same user take the test multiple times?

A：

Yes, users can take the DCEC test as many times as they wish in order to track any changes in their reading abilities. However, users are recommended to wait at least six months before retaking the test, as changes in their reading ability are predominantly influenced by their reading practices.
