SRE values correspond to the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR), the standard for language proficiency assessments in Europe. What is CEFR?

The Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR) is an international standard for describing proficiency in foreign languages including English and other European languages. CEFR categorizes proficiency into six levels: A1 (Beginners), A2 (Pre-Intermediate), B1 (Intermediate), B2 (Upper-intermediate), C1 (Advanced) and C2 (Proficiency). This framework is widely used as a reference by various major international language proficiency tests such as IELTS, TOEFL and GEPT, making CEFR a valuable standard for foreign language learners to assess their own language abilities.

What is Lexile?

Lexile is a measuring system developed by American science and education research institute MetaMetrics. The Lexile framework adopts a scientific method to evaluate reading ability and text readability. Lexile rates the difficulty level of a text according to its semantic difficulty and syntactic complexity on a scale from 0L to 2000L. Readers can use Lexile to identify their own reading ability and select reading materials best suited to their needs. For example, texts that are rated 5L and under are generally assessed to be suitable for Beginning Reader (BR) learners. Whether its English learners, teachers, or parents, everyone can utilize Lexile to select suitable texts for improving reading abilities.
